# ResonanceBreach — Initial Species Notes

## Goal

Define a first small ecological cast with enough difference to create meaningful interaction.

This is not the final bestiary.
It is the first canonical species set for the Resonance Garden.

---

## Species 01 — Rooted Bloom

### Role
Primary vegetal life

### Visual language
- rooted base
- thin stem or line
- crown / halo / clustered top
- calm, static, hand-drawn feel

### Behavior
- anchored to terrain
- grows slowly through stages
- emits subtle nutrient field
- may produce fruit when healthy
- contributes harmonic drone layer

### Needs
- nutrients
- suitable terrain
- optional pollination for richer reproduction

### Lifecycle
seedling → mature bloom → fruiting bloom → decay residue

---

## Species 02 — Pollinator Drifter

### Role
Small moving helper species

### Visual language
- light wing-like or petal-like form
- curved trajectory
- dotted motion traces
- agile but not chaotic

### Behavior
- attracted to blooms and fruit
- carries pollination between vegetal entities
- moves in loose swarms or pairs
- contributes short chirps / flutter tones

### Needs
- nearby plant life
- safe terrain
- access to nectar/fruit-like resources

### Lifecycle
larva/simple form → active drifter → reproductive pairing → death/residue

---

## Species 03 — Grazer

### Role
Fruit and soft-growth consumer

### Visual language
- compact body
- slightly heavier than pollinator
- more deliberate motion
- can grow into more complex form over time

### Behavior
- seeks fruit and edible growth
- reproduces if energy threshold is reached
- may form small groups
- dies if food becomes scarce

### Needs
- fruit
- low-danger movement space
- moderate nutrient-rich ecosystems

### Lifecycle
juvenile grazer → mature grazer → breeding stage → decline → corpse/residue

---

## Species 04 — Decomposer

### Role
Converts death into fertility

### Visual language
- low-profile, subtle, maybe filamentous or crawling
- less prominent than grazers or pollinators
- should feel essential but not flashy

### Behavior
- seeks residue/corpses
- consumes decay
- enriches nutrient field
- may reproduce asexually near rich residue

### Needs
- dead matter
- moist or nutrient-dense terrain

### Lifecycle
spore/simple form → active decomposition stage → spread/spawn → decline

---

## Species 05 — Predator (later phase)
Not required for immediate implementation, but part of the plan.

### Role
Population regulator and tension source

### Visual language
- more solitary
- sharper / more intentional motion
- stronger presence

### Behavior
- hunts grazers and maybe pollinators
- low numbers
- stronger individual salience in sound and rendering

---

## Reproduction Modes

### Asexual
Used by some vegetal and decomposer species.
- split
- seed
- spore
- budding

### Sexual
Used by some mobile or advanced species.
- requires pairing
- increases diversity
- can influence later evolution or mutation systems

---

## Growth and Morphology

Species should not only scale in size.
They should gain structure.

Examples:
- more appendages
- more branching
- changed crown
- different sound profile
- changed movement pattern

This should reflect maturity.

---

## Residue and Nutrient Logic

Death should not equal deletion.

### After death:
- body lingers briefly
- converts to residue particles or stain
- enriches local nutrient field
- may attract decomposers
