# ResonanceBreach Species Notes

## Purpose

This document defines the initial species direction for the modular architecture phase. These species are design anchors, not a demand to fully implement every behavior immediately.

## Core ecological rule

Death should not be a hard delete in design terms. Death becomes residue, and residue becomes nutrients that can support later life.

## Initial and near-term species

### Rooted Bloom

**Role**
- primary rooted producer
- converts fertile conditions into visible growth and fruit

**Behavior direction**
- anchored to place
- slow lifecycle progression
- fruiting when healthy and mature
- stabilizing presence in both visuals and audio

### Pollinator Drifter

**Role**
- small mobile helper species
- links plant patches and future pollination mechanics

**Behavior direction**
- attracted to blooms, fruit, and safe fertile regions
- agile, light, and socially loose
- suitable for brighter higher-register sound accents

### Decomposer

**Role**
- turns residue and dead matter back into ecological value

**Behavior direction**
- seeks residue-rich areas
- amplifies nutrient return
- should be visually subtle but systemically important

### Later Grazer

**Role**
- consumes fruit or soft growth
- creates pressure on producer populations

**Behavior direction**
- slower and more deliberate than pollinators
- reproduces after successful feeding
- eventually becomes prey or competition for later species

### Later Predator

**Role**
- regulates population and introduces selective tension

**Behavior direction**
- sparse but salient
- stronger threat signature in rendering and audio
- should remain readable and low-count

## Lifecycle framing

Species should be modeled around lifecycle transitions rather than only movement archetypes.

Suggested recurring stages:

- birth / emergence
- growth
- mature activity
- decline or decay
- residue / nutrient return

## Architecture guidance

As species modules are split out later, each species should eventually describe:

- spawn preferences
- terrain affinities
- field sensitivities
- feeding rules
- reproduction rules
- death and residue contribution
- audio salience hints
- rendering hints

That structure should support future ecosystem work without re-entangling simulation, rendering, and audio.
